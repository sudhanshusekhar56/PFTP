About

Virender Sehwag has constructed an extraordinary 16 years lasting career with a relentless quest, and a genius, for boundary hitting.
With minimal footwork but maximum intent, he has piled Test runs at a faster pace than anyone in the history of cricket.
Bowlers must always fancy their chances against a batsman who plays so many strokes; it's just that Sehwag fancies his chances against them much more.

Domestic Career

His uncomplicated approach - batting is all about scoring as many runs as quickly as possible -
belies a sharp and street-smart cricket mind. He has a keen grasp of his own, and his opponents',
strengths and weaknesses and exploits them in a forensic manner. What appears risky to many, is merely an
opportunity for him, and his lack of footwork, which does get him in trouble against the moving ball,
is mostly an advantage, for it creates space for his brilliant handwork. Few batsmen have hit the ball harder square on the off side.
